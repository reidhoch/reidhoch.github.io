Accidents & Emergencies

Indiana Driver's Manual

Among all collisions in Indiana, failure to yield the right of way is the most common factor. Accidents are often caused by a driver's lack of attention or failure to observe the rules of the road.

What To Do After an Accident

Legal Requirements

1

Stop Immediately

Stop as close to the scene as possible without obstructing traffic.

2

Remain at the Scene

Stay and provide your name, address, registration, and show your driver's license.

3

Provide Assistance

Help injured persons as directed by law enforcement, medical personnel, or 911.

4

Contact Law Enforcement

Notify police, sheriff, or state police as soon as possible.

Do NOT move if there is injury, death, entrapment, or hazardous materials.

If a Collision is Unavoidable

  • ‚Ä¢ Turn away from oncoming traffic, even if it means leaving the road
  • ‚Ä¢ Drive off the road rather than skid ‚Äî you'll have more control
  • ‚Ä¢ Hit something that will give way (brush, shrubs) rather than something hard
  • ‚Ä¢ Hit something moving in your direction rather than stationary
  • ‚Ä¢ A sideswipe is safer than a head-on collision

Wheels Off the Pavement

Do NOT turn back onto the pavement right away ‚Äî this could throw your vehicle off balance.

Instead: Drive along the shoulder, ease off the gas, slow down, then turn back onto the road gradually.

Vehicle in Water

A vehicle will usually float for several minutes before sinking.

  1. Do NOT open the door ‚Äî water pressure makes it nearly impossible and will flood the vehicle
  2. Remove your seat belt
  3. Open a window (automatic windows may still work)
  4. Get children out of restraints, move everyone to front seats
  5. Exit through window and move to the roof
  6. Call 911, locate nearest dry land
  7. Swim for shore only as a last resort
  8. Never re-enter the vehicle for possessions

Vehicle Equipment Failures

Tire Blowout

  1. 1. Hold the steering wheel firmly and keep the car straight
  2. 2. Take foot off gas ‚Äî do NOT brake
  3. 3. Let the car slow down gradually
  4. 4. Pull off the road
  5. 5. Apply brakes only when the car is almost stopped

Brake Failure

  1. 1. Shift to a lower gear
  2. 2. Pump the brake pedal fast and hard ‚Äî this may build pressure
  3. 3. Try the parking brake ‚Äî hold the release to avoid skidding
  4. 4. Look for a safe place to stop off the road
  5. 5. After stopping, call for help ‚Äî do not drive

Never Turn Off the Ignition While Moving

On vehicles with steering wheel interlock systems, turning the ignition to "lock" while moving will cause the steering to lock when you turn the wheel, resulting in loss of control.

Traffic Stops by Law Enforcement

What To Do When Pulled Over

1

Acknowledge with Right Turn Signal

This lets the officer know you see them.

2

Pull to the Right Shoulder

Never stop on the center median. Find a well-lit area if possible.

3

Turn Off Radio, End Phone Calls

Give the officer your full attention.

4

Stay in Vehicle, Hands Visible

Driver: hands on steering wheel. Passengers: hands on laps.

5

Roll Down Tinted Windows

If windows are tinted, roll them down before officer approaches.

6

Inform Before Reaching

Tell the officer where your documents are before reaching for them.

If You Cannot Pull Over Immediately

Slow down and turn on your hazard lights to show you acknowledge the officer and are looking for a safe place to stop.

Do NOT

  • ‚Ä¢ Exit your vehicle unless directed by the officer
  • ‚Ä¢ Argue or debate with the officer at the scene
  • ‚Ä¢ Refuse to sign a ticket (signing is not an admission of guilt)
  • ‚Ä¢ Refuse breath, blood, or performance tests (can result in license suspension)
  • ‚Ä¢ Resist arrest if taken into custody

Carbon Monoxide Poisoning

You cannot see, smell, or taste carbon monoxide.

Symptoms

  • ‚Ä¢ Drowsiness or dizziness
  • ‚Ä¢ Bluish tinge to skin or lips
  • ‚Ä¢ Headache
  • ‚Ä¢ Increased sensitivity to light

Prevention

  • ‚Ä¢ Have exhaust system checked regularly
  • ‚Ä¢ Be alert for unusual roar from under the car
  • ‚Ä¢ Never run engine in a closed garage
  • ‚Ä¢ In heavy traffic, close the fresh-air vent
  • ‚Ä¢ On highways in cold weather, open the fresh-air vent

  • ‚Ä¢ Remove keys and always lock doors
  • ‚Ä¢ Do not hide a second set of keys in or around the vehicle
  • ‚Ä¢ Park with front wheels turned sharply and apply emergency brake
  • ‚Ä¢ Never leave vehicle running unattended
  • ‚Ä¢ Consider installing an anti-theft device
  • ‚Ä¢ Park in well-lit, well-patrolled areas
