CH 6

Traffic Signs & Signals

Indiana Driver's Manual

Traffic signs control traffic flow, making streets and highways safe for drivers, bicyclists, and pedestrians. Signs use colors, shapes, written messages, and symbols to help drivers quickly understand information.

Sign Colors

The background color identifies the type of information displayed.

Red

Stop, yield, or prohibited actions. Requires immediate action to avoid threats to safety.

Orange

Temporary traffic conditions — construction zones and maintenance projects.

Yellow

Warning — prepares drivers for specific road conditions and hazards ahead.

Fluorescent Yellow-Green

Schools, pedestrians, bicycles, playgrounds, and school bus routes.

Green

Permitted movements, directions, guidance — highway entrances/exits, distances.

Blue

Road services and information — amenities at upcoming exits.

Brown

Recreational and cultural interest sites — state parks, historical markers.

White

Traffic regulations that drivers must obey — speed limits, "No Turn on Red."

Sign Shapes

The shape also indicates the type of information displayed.

Octagon (8-sided)

Stop — you must stop and yield the appropriate right of way.

Triangle (pointing down)

Yield — slow down, be prepared to stop for other vehicles or pedestrians.

Circle

Railroad crossing ahead — be alert for trains.

Diamond

Warning — upcoming road conditions and hazards.

Pentagon (5-sided)

School zone — children may be crossing the road.

Pennant

No passing zone — posted on left side of two-way roads.

Rectangle

Regulations, route markers, destination info, or advisory speeds.

Railroad Signs

Circular yellow signs and crossbuck (X-shaped) signs alert drivers to upcoming railroad crossings. Always be prepared to stop.

Traffic Signals

Steady Lights

Green

Go — you have the right of way if the intersection is clear.

Yellow

Caution — signal is about to turn red. Stop if safe to do so.

Red

Stop — wait until the light turns green and intersection is clear.

Flashing Lights

Flashing Yellow

Slow down and proceed with caution. Yield to cross traffic and pedestrians.

Flashing Red

Same as a stop sign — come to a complete stop, then proceed when safe.

Turning on Red

  • Right on red: Permitted after a complete stop, when safe, unless a "No Turn on Red" sign is posted.
  • Left on red: Only when turning from a one-way street onto another one-way street, after stopping.

Arrow Signals

  • Green arrow: You have the right of way to turn in the direction of the arrow.
  • Yellow arrow: Your protected turn is ending.
  • Yellow flashing arrow: You may turn after yielding to pedestrians and oncoming traffic.
  • Red arrow: Do not turn in that direction until the arrow turns green.

Pedestrian Signals

WALK
Walk / Walking Person Symbol

Pedestrians may safely cross the street.

DON'T WALK
Don't Walk / Raised Hand Symbol

Not safe to cross. Flashing means time to cross is ending.

Pedestrian Hybrid Beacon

Found at mid-block crosswalks. Dark until activated by a pedestrian.

  1. Dark until activated
  2. Flashing yellow — prepare to stop
  3. Steady yellow — stop if safe
  4. Steady red — stop for pedestrians
  5. Flashing red — proceed if crossing is clear
  6. Returns to dark

Key Rules to Remember

At a stop sign: Stop at the white line. If none, stop before the crosswalk. If no crosswalk, stop before entering the intersection.

At a yield sign: Slow down and be prepared to stop. Traffic you're yielding to should not have to brake to avoid you.

Non-working signal: Treat as a four-way stop. Yield to vehicles already in intersection, vehicles that stopped first, and vehicles to your right.

Slow-moving vehicle emblem: Orange fluorescent triangle with red reflective border indicates vehicles that cannot exceed 25 mph.
