CH 7

Safe Vehicle Operation

Chapter 7 — Indiana Driver's Manual

A defensive driver looks out for the actions of other drivers and anticipates potential problems. This chapter covers lane markings, speed limits, passing rules, and how to safely share the road.

Lane Markings

Yellow Lines

Separate traffic going in opposite directions.

  • Broken yellow: You may cross to pass when safe.
  • Solid yellow on your side: Do not cross except to turn.
  • Double solid yellow: Neither direction may pass.

White Lines

Separate traffic going in the same direction.

  • Broken white: You may change lanes when safe.
  • Solid white: Do not cross — stay in your lane.

Changing Lanes

  1. Check that the lane ahead is clear
  2. Check mirrors for vehicles preparing to pass
  3. Turn head briefly to check blind spot
  4. Signal your intention
  5. Move smoothly into the new lane

Important: Change only one lane at a time. Do not weave in and out of traffic.

Passing Other Vehicles

How to Pass Safely

  • • Make sure passing lane is clear — return to your lane at least 100 feet before any oncoming vehicle
  • • Check behind and to the left for vehicles passing you
  • • Signal, move into passing lane, accelerate
  • • Continue until you see the passed vehicle in your rearview mirror
  • • Signal and return to your lane

Passing is Prohibited

  • • Solid yellow line on your side
  • • "No Passing Zone" pennant posted
  • • On or approaching a curve
  • • Approaching a hill crest
  • • Within 100 feet of intersections, railroad crossings, bridges, or tunnels

Being Passed: Allow the other vehicle to pass safely. Do not increase your speed.

School Zones

Slow to posted limit. Common hours: 7 AM – 4:30 PM on school days, or when beacons are flashing.

Work Zones

Always at least 10 mph below the maximum speed limit for the area.

School Bus Speed Limits

  • • Non-interstate/highway: 40 mph max (or posted limit if lower)
  • • Interstate/highway: 60 mph max (or posted limit)

Following Distance & Braking

2-3 Second Rule

Stay at least 2-3 seconds behind the vehicle ahead. Watch for it to pass a fixed object, then count the time until you pass the same object.

Skid Recovery

Standard Brakes:

Ease off gas. Steer in the direction the rear is sliding. When you regain traction, straighten and proceed slowly.

ABS Brakes:

Keep foot on brake with firm, continuous pressure. Steer normally. Do not pump the brakes.

Driving in Weather Conditions

Rain

  • • Roads are most slippery at the start of rainfall
  • • Hydroplaning occurs when tires lose contact with the road due to water
  • • Slow down, avoid bald tires, test brakes after puddles

Fog

  • • Use low beam headlights (not high beams)
  • • Reduce speed, use emergency flashers
  • • If visibility is near zero, pull off road and stop

Winter / Ice

  • • Clear all windows before driving
  • • Watch for ice on bridges and overpasses (freezes first)
  • • Stay behind snowplows at safe distance
  • • Keep gas tank full in case stranded

Flash Flooding

  • • Do not drive around barricades or through flooded roads
  • • Water depth can be deceiving and may hide washouts
  • • If car stalls in flood water, abandon it immediately

Driving at Night

Headlight Rules

  • • Use headlights sunset to sunrise and when visibility is less than 500 feet
  • • Use low beams within 500 feet of oncoming traffic
  • • Use low beams when following within 200 feet of another vehicle
  • • To avoid glare, look at the right side of the road, not at oncoming lights

Impaired & Distracted Driving

Drowsy Driving

Going 18 hours without sleep impairs you as much as a 0.08 BAC (the legal limit).

Warning signs:

• Yawning, heavy eyelids
• Head nodding
• Missing exits/signs
• Hitting rumble strips
• Can't remember last few miles
• Changing lanes unexpectedly

Distracted Driving — Indiana Law

It is illegal to use a cell phone while driving unless it is hands-free. Texting while driving is illegal.

Texting at 55 mph = driving the length of a football field with your eyes closed.

Aggressive Driving

Defined as committing 3 or more of the following in one episode:

• Following too closely
• Unsafe operation
• Passing on right off-road
• Unsafe stopping/slowing
• Unnecessary horn use
• Failure to yield
• Failure to obey signals
• Unsafe speed
• Repeatedly flashing lights

Sharing the Road

Move Over Law

When approaching stopped emergency or service vehicles with flashing lights:

  • • Change lanes away from the vehicle if possible
  • • If you cannot move over, slow to 10 mph under the posted limit

Applies to: emergency vehicles, tow trucks, highway maintenance, utility vehicles, trash trucks, and disabled vehicles with hazards on.

School Buses

  • • Stop when red lights flash and stop arm is extended
  • • On divided highway: stop only if traveling in same direction
  • • Watch for children who may dart into the street
  • • School buses stop at railroad crossings — don't rear-end them!

Tractor-Trailers

  • Stopping distance: 4x longer than cars (400+ feet at 55 mph vs ~130 feet for cars)
  • Blind spots: 20 feet in front, alongside cab, either side of trailer, up to 200 feet behind
  • Turning: Trucks swing wide — don't pass on the right when they're turning

If you can't see their mirrors, they can't see you.

Bicycles

  • • Pass with at least 3 feet of space
  • • Bicyclists have the same rights as motor vehicles
  • • Bicyclists may use travel lane or bike lane
  • • Check for bicyclists before opening car doors

Motorcycles

  • • Allow full lane width — don't share lanes
  • • Harder to judge speed and distance due to small size
  • • Turn signals are not self-canceling — wait to confirm turn
  • • Allow 3-4 seconds following distance

Railroad Crossings

Vehicles Required to Stop

Must stop between 15-50 feet from the nearest rail:

  • • All vehicles carrying passengers for hire
  • • All school buses
  • • Vehicles carrying explosives or flammable liquids

Railroad Safety Rules

  • • Never drive around a lowered gate
  • • Never race a train — their speed is deceiving
  • • Don't stop or shift gears on the tracks
  • • If stalled on tracks: evacuate immediately, run at 45° angle toward the train
  • • Do not pass within 100 feet of a crossing
  • • Watch for second trains on multiple tracks

Roundabouts & Intersections

Roundabouts

  • • Incoming traffic yields to circulating traffic
  • • Traffic moves counterclockwise
  • • Know your lane before entering — don't change lanes inside
  • • Yield to large vehicles (trucks, buses) that may need multiple lanes

All-Way Stops

  • • First to stop = first to go
  • • If arriving at the same time, vehicle on the left yields to vehicle on the right
  • • When in doubt, yield to avoid a crash

Parking

Wheel Position on Hills

Downhill (with curb):

Turn wheels toward the curb

Uphill (with curb):

Turn wheels away from curb

No curb? Turn wheels away from the street.

Illegal Parking

• Within 15 feet of fire hydrant
• On sidewalks
• In front of driveways
• Within intersections
• On bridges/tunnels
• Beside yellow curbs
• In crosswalks
• Beside another parked car

Seat Belts & Child Restraints

Indiana Law

  • • All drivers and passengers must wear seat belts
  • • Children under 8 must be in a child restraint system
  • • Children under 12 should ride in the back seat (air bag danger)

Seat Belt Tips

  • • Wear belt low on hips and flat across collar bone
  • • Driver should sit at least 12 inches from steering wheel
  • • Seat belts increase survival chance by 45%

Pedestrian Safety

  • • Always yield to pedestrians
  • • Don't make a turn that causes a pedestrian to stop or take evasive action
  • • Take special care around children — they may cross unexpectedly
  • • Always yield to visually impaired pedestrians (white cane or guide dog)
